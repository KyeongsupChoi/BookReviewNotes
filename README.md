## To Read List

🧠 1. Core Machine Learning Foundations

1. Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning by Christopher M. Bishop
The classic graduate-level text — builds rigorous intuition in probability, models, and inference.

🔹 Heavy math, but irreplaceable for solid theoretical grounding.

2. Machine Learning: A Probabilistic Perspective by Kevin P. Murphy
Comprehensive and modern — covers graphical models, Bayesian reasoning, and deep learning foundations.

🔹 Great bridge between math, algorithms, and real-world ML.

🧩 2. Deep Learning & Modern AI

3. Deep Learning by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, & Aaron Courville
The “Bible” of deep learning — explains the theory behind neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, optimization, and representation learning.

4. Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras, and TensorFlow by Aurélien Géron
A practical guide to implementing modern ML and DL systems using Python.

🔹 Perfect for getting your hands dirty with real code.

📊 3. Data & Statistical Thinking

5. The Elements of Statistical Learning by Hastie, Tibshirani, & Friedman
Focuses on the mathematical and statistical principles behind ML algorithms — a must for anyone going beyond plug-and-play ML.

6. An Introduction to Statistical Learning (ISL) by James, Witten, Hastie, & Tibshirani
A lighter, more practical companion to the above — includes R/Python examples.

🔹 Ideal for grasping core statistical learning concepts.

⚙️ 4. Systems, Deployment & Scaling

7. Designing Machine Learning Systems by Chip Huyen
Modern and concise — teaches how to turn ML prototypes into production-ready, maintainable systems.

🔹 Great for ML engineers working at the intersection of research and production.

8. Building Machine Learning Powered Applications by Emmanuel Ameisen
Covers the full ML lifecycle: scoping, experimentation, evaluation, deployment, and monitoring.

🔹 Perfect for practical MLOps mindset.

🧩 5. Broader AI & Reasoning

9. Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach by Stuart Russell & Peter Norvig
The foundational AI textbook — covers search, logic, reasoning, knowledge representation, and the philosophy of AI.

🔹 Ideal for understanding “AI” beyond just ML.

10. Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction by Richard S. Sutton & Andrew G. Barto
The definitive guide to RL theory and algorithms — from basic bandits to policy gradients and deep RL.

💬 Bonus Picks (for the curious engineer)

Introduction to Machine Learning with Python — Andreas Müller & Sarah Guido

Deep Reinforcement Learning Hands-On — Maxim Lapan

Practical Deep Learning for Cloud, Mobile, and Edge — Anirudh Koul et al.

You Look Like a Thing and I Love You — Janelle Shane (fun & ethical intro to AI behavior)
